# How to Profit from Flash Loan Arbitrage - Sahm Capital

## What Are Flash Loans?

Flash loans are uncollateralized DeFi borrowing mechanisms allowing users to borrow cryptocurrencies from on-chain liquidity pools, with repayment required within a single blockchain transaction.

**Key Characteristic:** "If the borrower fails to do that, the transaction is completely reverted. Risk of default is zero."

## How Arbitrage Works

The strategy exploits price discrepancies across decentralized exchanges (DEXs).

**Example:** If ETH trades at $3,000 on Uniswap but $3,001 on SushiSwap, traders can buy low and sell high simultaneously within seconds.

## Profit Mechanism - Concrete Example

The article illustrates a detailed arbitrage scenario:

1. **Borrow:** $500K in USDC via flash loan
2. **Buy:** DOGE at $0.50 on Uniswap = 1M DOGE tokens
3. **Sell:** 1M DOGE at $0.60 on SushiSwap = $600K
4. **Repay:** $500K + Aave's 0.05% fee ($250)
5. **Net Profit:** Approximately $75K

## Critical Risks

### Technical Complexity
- Requires coding knowledge and smart contract expertise
- High technical barrier to entry

### Minimal Margins
- Success dependent on microsecond execution speed
- Competition reduces profit opportunities

### Smart Contract Vulnerabilities
- Security risks in contract code
- **Real Example:** Hackers exploited flash loans to attack Alpha Homora, stealing $37 million

### Gas Fee Volatility
- Ethereum gas fees can eliminate thin profit margins
- Network congestion impacts profitability

## Reality Check

**2023 Case Study:** An Ethereum bot executed a $200M flash loan transaction for merely $3.24 profit, illustrating the difficulty of achieving meaningful returns despite massive capital deployment.

## Key Takeaway

While flash loan arbitrage offers theoretical profit opportunities with zero initial capital, the reality involves:
- Extreme competition
- Razor-thin margins
- High technical requirements
- Significant security risks
- Operational complexity that often results in minimal actual profits
